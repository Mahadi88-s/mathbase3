To calculate the perimeter using the Pythagorean Theorem, you need to have a right-angled triangle, which is a triangle with one angle measuring 90 degrees. The Pythagorean Theorem states that in a right-angled triangle, the square of the hypotenuse (the side opposite the right angle) is equal to the sum of the squares of the other two sides. 1. Identify the right-angled triangle for which you want to calculate the perimeter. 2. Determine the lengths of the two shorter sides of the triangle. 3. Square each of these lengths. 4. Add the two squared side lengths together. 5. Take the square root of the sum obtained in step 4 to find the length of the hypotenuse. 6. Finally, add the lengths of all three sides of the triangle to find the perimeter. Here's an example: Suppose you have a right-angled triangle with side lengths of 3 units and 4 units. You want to find the perimeter. 1. Identify the right-angled triangle. 2. The two shorter sides have lengths of 3 units and 4 units. 3. Square 3 units: 3^2 = 9. Square 4 units: 4^2 = 16. 4.
